Buying crypto in the US: what you need to know

Regulation matters

US-regulated exchanges register as Money Service Businesses with FinCEN and comply with state-level money transmitter licenses. Some states like New York require a separate BitLicense. Regulated exchanges provide 1099 tax forms and FDIC insurance on USD deposits.

Tax reporting

The IRS treats crypto as property. Every trade, swap, and sale is a taxable event. Choose an exchange that provides clear transaction history and integrates with tax software like TurboTax, CoinTracker, or Koinly.

Payment methods

ACH bank transfers are free on most US exchanges. Wire transfers are faster but cost $10-25. Debit/credit cards work but carry 2-4% fees. Some exchanges now support Apple Pay and PayPal.
